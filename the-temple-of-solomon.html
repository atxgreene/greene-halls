The Temple of Solomon

בֵּית הַמִּקְדָּשׁ

A walkable replica of the First Temple — the pillars Jachin and Boaz, the Menorah, the veiled Holy of Holies — and an archive of the Solomon legend: the Seal, the Testament, the Lesser Key, and the Tree of Life. This hall presents scripture, history, legend, Kabbalah, and Renaissance occult literature as cultural and mythic history. It is not instruction, not ritual, and makes no claim that magic, summoning, or curses are real or effective. Walk with curiosity and discernment.

The Temple Could Not Be Raised

This hall builds a real-time 3D chamber and needs WebGL plus a one-time download of the rendering engine from a CDN. If you are offline or your browser blocks WebGL, the immersive temple cannot start.

The Stations of the Temple

Where to Walk

Eight stations ring the court and the house. Choose one to read its scripture, history, and — clearly marked apart — its legend and esoteric tradition.

Discernment & Return

How to Hold This Hall

The museum keeps measured fact and metaphor apart on purpose. So should you.

The registers, kept separate

Scripture the Temple as the Hebrew Bible describes it (1 Kings 6–7; 2 Chronicles 3–4).

History what archaeology and historians can and cannot confirm — the First Temple itself is not directly attested in the archaeological record.

Legend later stories: the Testament of Solomon, the signet ring, Hiram Abiff.

Esoteric Kabbalah and the Tree of Life — a living mystical tradition.

Occult literature Renaissance grimoires (the Lesser Key of Solomon). Studied here as literature and cultural history.

This hall is a museum exhibit. It presents myth, scripture, and occult texts — it is not a spell book, not instruction, and asserts no claim that ritual magic, summoning, sealing, or cursing is real or works. Take what is beautiful and historical; leave superstition at the door. If any of this unsettles you, step out to the porch and breathe.
