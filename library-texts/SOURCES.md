## ⚠️ Partial (1 book)

**Book 9 — Nag Hammadi PD Tractates** (`nag-hammadi-pd-tractates.txt`, 186,482 words)

The James M. Robinson translation of the Nag Hammadi Library (Harper & Row, 1977) is **under copyright** and was not included. Instead, this file contains G.R.S. Mead's *Fragments of a Faith Forgotten* (1900/1906), which covers the same Gnostic traditions—Naassenes, Valentinians, Basilideans, Marcosians, Peratae, Sethians, and others—with extensive quotations and analysis. This is the best available PD approximation. The file is labeled "partial" because it is a scholarly survey, not a direct translation of the Nag Hammadi codices themselves.

## ⛔ Skipped (4 entries)

### Book 3 — 3 Enoch (Sefer Hekhalot)
**Reason:** No pre-1928 PD English translation exists. Hugo Odeberg's standard translation was published in **1928** (not before 1928), placing it outside the US public domain threshold. Odeberg died 1981; copyright runs to approximately 2051. Philip Alexander's 1983 translation is also copyrighted. 3 Enoch was not known to Western scholarship before Odeberg's edition.

### Book 4 — The Book of Giants
**Reason:** No pre-1928 PD English translation exists. The Qumran fragments were not discovered until **1947**. The Manichaean fragments were identified by W.B. Henning in 1943 (UK copyright until ~2037). All scholarly translations are post-1947 and under copyright.  
*Note: A PDF titled "The Book Of The Giants.pdf" exists in the library at `Theology/Ancient Bible/The Book Of The Giants.pdf`. Its edition and license are unknown and it was not used as a source.*

### Book 15 — The Zohar (Manhar Partial Translation)
**Reason:** The Soncino Press translation (Sperling/Simon, 1931) is **copyrighted** and excluded per instructions. The Nurho de Manhar partial translation (pre-1928, covering Genesis/Exodus) is hosted at sacred-texts.com/jud/zdm/, but that site was **Cloudflare-blocked** for all programmatic access at the time of retrieval. The Wayback Machine has not archived those pages. No archive.org copy was found.  
*To access the Manhar translation: visit https://sacred-texts.com/jud/zdm/index.htm directly in a browser.*
